Hi, I'm Akash — a data engineer. This is the first post on my new site, so consider it a
placeholder-with-purpose: a marker for where the writing will live.

## Why a blog

Most of what I've learned about data engineering didn't come from documentation. It came from
pipelines that broke at the worst possible time, migrations that looked simple until the edge cases
showed up, and the slow realization that "reliable" is a feature you have to design for, not
something you get for free.

I want a place to write those lessons down.

## What to expect

A few themes I'll come back to:

- **Pipelines that don't break at 3am** — orchestration, retries, idempotency, and observability.
- **Warehousing** — modeling for Snowflake, dbt patterns, and keeping cost under control.
- **Migrations** — moving off legacy systems without losing history or trust.
- **The boring-but-important stuff** — data quality, testing, and documentation.
